DeOpenRouter

A transparent, low-trust-assumption AI API marketplace prototype: Solidity, Next.js, Hono, and FastAPI. Trust coordination, pricing, payments, and audit anchoring on-chain; model inference off-chain.

Background

The rise of centralized AI gateways

We are in an era of exploding LLMs and AI applications. To connect to various models, developers face complex APIs and billing systems.

  • Centralized AI Gateways (e.g. OpenRouter) emerged to solve this.
  • They act as aggregators, providing a unified API to route to different models and settle costs in one place.
  • This model significantly lowers integration costs and improves efficiency.
  • Current Reality: While AI capabilities grow, distribution and access heavily rely on centralized platforms.

Problem

Centralized AI gateways are trust-heavy

Typical routers consolidate pricing, routing, billing, and disputes under one operator. That is convenient, but users cannot easily verify quiet rule changes, whether a charge matched the published policy, or whether records could be rewritten after the fact.

  • Our narrative hook: which parts of the workflow belong on an EVM so rules and settlement are publicly observable and harder to tamper with?
  • We do not claim fully on-chain inference; we trade realism for clearer settlement and auditability.

Solutions

What we move on-chain

  • Provider identity commitments and metadata registered publicly
  • Price updates are not instantly effective; they have observable delay
  • Each paid call leaves an auditable on-chain record
  • Stake can be reduced via an on-chain slashing proposal with a challenge window; proceeds go to configurable slashTreasury
  • External audit outcomes can be anchored as report hashes

Inference stays off-chain for practicality; settlement and auditing become more transparent.

System

Our System contains

  • Smart contract marketplace: registration, stake, delayed price changes, per-call settlement, slashing, audit anchoring
  • Web app: separate user and provider flows; browse, register, invoke (some on-chain detail panels are MVP mock layouts)
  • Relay API: local mock or upstream OpenRouter proxy
  • Audit server: structured checks on the relay; summaries can be written back on-chain
  • MVP UI data model: certain marketplace/history views intentionally merge demo rows with live chain-backed rows

Work Flow

End-to-end journey

  1. Provider registers on-chain: price, stake, metadata URI, identity and capability hashes, endpointCommitment
  2. Web lists providers; users can simulate calls via the relay API
  3. Client hashes request and response, then calls on-chain invoke(...) with payment
  4. Contract applies delayed price when due, transfers fees, refunds overpayment, stores call record, emits CallRecorded
  5. If auditing is enabled: probe relay off-chain → canonical JSON → optional off-chain publish → recordAudit / recordAuditWithUri anchors keccak256(canonicalJson)

Governance

Who governs, how audits work, how slashing executes

Role model

slashOperator, auditRecorder, and slashTreasury are explicit on-chain roles. Critical role rotation is two-step: propose* → accept*.

Audit flow

Direct anchoring via recordAudit*, or round-based flow via beginAuditRound + allowlisted attestAudit. MVP quorum aggregation remains mostly off-chain.

  • Slashing lifecycle: proposeSlash → provider may challengeSlashProposal during challenge window → finalizeSlashProposal.
  • Fund destination: slashed ETH goes to configurable slashTreasury rather than defaulting to the operator.
  • Governance parameterization: challenge-period and treasury settings stay transparent and queryable on-chain.

Audit Capability

6D Risk matrix and on-chain anchoring

Comprehensive 6D checks

D1-D2: Hidden injection and instruction overrides.
D3-D4: Tool-call substitution (AC-1.a) and error leakage.
D5-D6: Stream integrity (SSE anomalies) and Web3 injection.

Risk matrix output

The FastAPI server evaluates probes and yields a definitive LOW, MEDIUM, or HIGH risk level, avoiding false positives via deterministic structural rules.

  • Execution API: POST /v1/audit on apps/audit-server returns structured JSON.
  • Canonical evidence: Report JSON is canonically serialized and hashed to produce deterministic, reproducible audit evidence.
  • On-chain anchoring: Relay scheduler calls recordAudit / recordAuditWithUri to write the reportHash and risk level on-chain.

Design intent: keep heavy audit computation off-chain, while making the final evidence and settlement-facing signal tamper-resistant on-chain.

Weakness and Future Development

Economic security

Economic safety still depends on multisig and treasury configuration. The chain implements slashing delay + challenge and auditor allowlisted multisig, but there is no full arbitration market, escrow settlement, or cryptographic proof of correct inference.
